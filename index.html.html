Capstone Project

Ohio Adult Obesity Analysis

A comprehensive county-level analysis of adult obesity prevalence across Ohio using CDC PLACES data to support data-driven public health decision-making.

Module 1 & 2 · 2025

Business Understanding & Data Preparation

Analyzing adult obesity prevalence across Ohio counties to identify high-risk regions, uncover geographic disparities, and support targeted public health interventions using CDC PLACES 2023 data.

🎯 Problem: How does adult obesity prevalence vary across Ohio counties to identify high-risk regions?
📦 Dataset: CDC PLACES – Local Data for Better Health (Census tract, 2023)
⚙️ Methods: Data filtering, county-level aggregation, cleaning & validation
🏥 Impact: Enables targeted healthcare resource allocation and policy planning

Research Design

Business Research Questions

Six structured, measurable questions that guide the analysis and ensure meaningful insights can be derived from the dataset.

01
Primary Question: How does adult obesity prevalence vary across counties in Ohio? This serves as the foundation for further analysis.
02
Which counties have the highest obesity prevalence? Identifies high-risk areas requiring targeted public health interventions.
03
Which counties have the lowest obesity prevalence? Provides comparison benchmarks and potential best practices.
04
What is the overall distribution of obesity rates? Examines spread vs. concentration of rates across the state.
05
Are obesity rates concentrated regionally or spread statewide? Reveals whether this is a localized or systemic issue.
06
What insights emerge from comparing extremes? Uncovers meaningful differences influencing policy decisions.

Methodology

Data Preparation Pipeline

A rigorous five-step process to ensure the dataset is accurate, consistent, and ready for county-level obesity analysis.

01

Data Filtering

Filtered the initial CDC dataset to include only Ohio records with the measure "Obesity among adults." This reduced noise and sharpened focus on the defined business problem, eliminating irrelevant states and health indicators.

02

Data Aggregation

The CDC PLACES dataset is organized at census tract level — multiple records per county. To ensure fair county comparisons, average obesity rates were calculated per county, giving each county exactly one representative value.

03

Data Cleaning

Reviewed and removed missing or null values in key fields. Verified obesity percentages fall within realistic ranges. Ensured consistent data types and formatting throughout to prevent errors during analysis.

04

Data Validation

Cross-checked obesity values across counties for logical consistency. Confirmed the distribution aligns with expected public health patterns. Investigated any unusual or extreme values that may indicate data entry errors.

05

Limitations Assessment

Documented key constraints: data contains modeled estimates (not direct measurements), only 2023 data was available after filtering, and the absence of multi-year data limits trend analysis capabilities.

Findings

Initial Insights

Preliminary observations from the dataset that set the stage for deeper analysis.

📊
Geographic Variation
Obesity rates are not consistent across Ohio counties. Significant variation exists between high-risk and low-risk regions, suggesting socioeconomic and environmental factors at play.
📍
Confidence Limit Variation
Confidence limits vary across counties, indicating that some estimates are more uncertain than others. This must be considered carefully when interpreting comparative results.
🏥
Actionable for Policy
The data reveals clear opportunities for targeted healthcare resource allocation, awareness campaigns, and fitness initiatives in counties with high obesity concentrations.
